The Extent of Social Media Usage

To understand how social media impacts us, we must first understand the extent to which we're collectively using social media. Pew Research regularly conducts surveys regarding this topic and below are the results of some of their most recent surveys.

The Invisible Brain Chemistry Changes

A research paper from the University of Liberal Arts Bangladesh, surveyed social media users to understand their social media use and mental health conditions. Two of the questions asked in this survey were: “How frequently do you experience feelings of anxiety?” and ”How frequently do you experience feelings of depression?”. Users were then prompted to answer the questions utilizing the following scale: 1 - Never, 2 - Rarely, 3 - Sometimes, 4 - Often, and 5 - Almost Always.

How to explore: The following visualization will help you take a closer look at the distribution of answers across survey respondents. Hover over the categories to observe the relationship between daily social media usage and feelings of depression and anxiety.

Platform Personalities

Let’s dive deeper into the relationship between social media and mental health by exploring the repeated behavioral changes that lead to anxiety or depression as noted on the homepage. However, not every social media platform is designed the same way and each results in differing user behavior. We wanted to observe whether user behavior changes depending on their preferred social media platforms based on the same users as the previous page.

To do so, we have divided the platforms into two categories. Personal Media Sharing platforms include: Facebook, Instagram, Youtube, Tiktok and Snapchat. Bookmarking or Discussion platforms include: Discord, Reddit, Twitter and Pinterest.

Users were then asked to rate, on a scale of 1 (Never) to 5 (Almost Always), how often they experience the following feelings when not actively online: Comparison Feelings, Seeking Validation Feelings, Distracted Feelings, and Restless Feelings. We have named them: Comparison Rating, Validation Rating, Distractibility Rating, and Restless Rating, respectively. We then took the average of each metric for each platform and platform category.

How to explore: The average rating will impact the size of the bubbles you see below. You can compare both categories and platforms and see how they affect the different mental health metrics.
